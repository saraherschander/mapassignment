In Queens, City-Run Childcare Centers are Few and Far Between

December 10, 2020

By Sara Herschander

This September, NYC launched its new Learning Bridges initiative, designed to provide childcare for the city's essential workers and most vulnerable families in light of an uncertain 2020-2021 school year. Yet the rollout has been far from equitable.

"The [COVID-19] crisis is compounded by the dearth of affordable childcare. The absense of affordable child care leaves families with young children facing significant strain to maintain stable epmloyment or re-enter the workforce," said Jennifer March, Executive Director of Citizens' Committee for Children (CCC).

A study from the CCC found that only 1% of families in Elmhurst and Corona can afford private center-based childcare and only 6% could afford home-based care. With zero Learning Bridges centers located in Elmhurst, Corona, or neighboring Jackson Heights, working families in Central Queens have no close options for childcare.

They're not alone. Queens has by far the fewest Learning Bridges locations per capita in New York City, with 40, 247 people per location.

Queens, NY has comparatively few Learning Bridges centers and several neighborhoods have no centers at all.



Residents of Queens, NY have nearly 1/2 as many Learning Bridges locations per capita than residents of other boroughs.
